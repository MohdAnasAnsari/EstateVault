# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy

**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Policy Owner:** Compliance Officer, EstateVault DMCC

---

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes EstateVault DMCC's ("VAULT") framework for complying with anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), and KYC obligations. It applies to:

- All VAULT users seeking Level 2 or Level 3 platform access
- All property listings and transactions facilitated via the Platform
- All VAULT employees, contractors, and agents

VAULT operates in compliance with:
- **UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018** on Anti-Money Laundering and Combating the Financing of Terrorism
- **UAE Cabinet Decision No. 10 of 2019** (Implementing Regulation)
- **FATF Recommendations** (40 Recommendations + R9)
- **EU AMLD6** (for EU-connected transactions)
- **DIFC AML Rules** (where applicable)

---

## 2. Risk-Based Approach

VAULT applies a risk-based approach to AML/CTF compliance. All users are assessed based on:

| Risk Factor | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nationality | Low-risk FATF countries | Non-FATF countries | FATF black/grey list countries |
| Transaction value | < AED 500,000 | AED 500K – 10M | > AED 10M |
| PEP status | No match | Close associate | Direct PEP |
| Sanctions | No match | Indirect link | Direct match |
| Business structure | Individual | Private company | Complex structures, trusts |
| Geography | UAE/GCC | OECD | High-risk jurisdictions |

## 3. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 3.1 Standard CDD (Level 2 Access)
All users seeking verified access must provide:
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID, or driving licence)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement — dated within 3 months)
- Self-declaration of source of funds category

Identity documents are verified electronically via our KYC partner (Jumio) using AI-assisted liveness detection and document authenticity checks.

### 3.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD — Level 3 Access)
High-risk users or those transacting above AED 5 million are subject to EDD:
- Certified copies of identity documents
- Source of wealth declaration and supporting evidence
- Bank reference letters
- Corporate structure documentation (for entities)
- Beneficial ownership declarations (>25% ownership)

### 3.3 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
PEPs and their immediate family members or close associates are automatically escalated to Enhanced Due Diligence regardless of transaction value. Senior management approval is required before onboarding any PEP.

### 3.4 Sanctions Screening
All users are screened against:
- UN Security Council Sanctions List
- EU Consolidated Sanctions List
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- UAE Local Terrorist List (Cabinet Decision No. 83 of 2021)
- HM Treasury Consolidated Sanctions List

Screening is performed at onboarding and upon any material change to user status. Positive matches are automatically escalated to the Compliance Officer and the user is blocked from Platform activity pending review.

## 4. Ongoing Monitoring

VAULT's platform performs continuous transaction monitoring for:
- **Unusual price patterns:** Listings priced significantly above or below market value
- **Structuring:** Multiple deals from the same beneficial owner designed to avoid thresholds
- **Round-tripping:** Funds moving in and out of the same jurisdiction within short periods
- **Anonymous parties:** Attempts to obscure true beneficial ownership

AI-powered fraud detection flags listings and deal patterns that match known AML typologies for manual review by the Compliance Officer.

---

## 5. Suspicious Activity Reporting

### 5.1 Internal Reporting
Any VAULT employee or the automated monitoring system that identifies suspicious activity must report it to the **Compliance Officer** within **24 hours** using the internal SAR form.

### 5.2 External Reporting — UAE
Suspicious transactions are reported to:
- **UAE Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU)** via the **goAML platform** within **the legally required timeframe** (immediately upon suspicion)

### 5.3 Tipping-Off Prohibition
VAULT employees and automated systems must not disclose to any person that a suspicious activity report has been filed or is being considered (tipping-off prohibition per UAE AML Law Art. 16).

### 5.4 Record Keeping
All AML records, screening results, SARs, and CDD documentation are retained for **10 years** from the date of the last transaction or account closure.

---

## 6. Real Estate-Specific Red Flags

VAULT monitors for the following real estate AML typologies:
- Cash purchases above AED 55,000 (UAE real estate reporting threshold)
- Purchase price significantly deviating from valuations (>20% above/below comparable sales)
- Third-party payments (payment by person not party to the transaction)
- Rapid re-sale of recently acquired property
- Reluctance to provide source of funds documentation
- Complex ownership structures with no apparent commercial purpose
- Deals with parties in FATF-identified high-risk jurisdictions
